# Code of Conduct

## 1. Introduction

This Code of Conduct outlines the behavioral expectations for all employees. Our company is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and inclusive workplace.

## 2. Core Values

### 2.1 Integrity
- Act honestly and ethically in all business dealings
- Report any unethical behavior through proper channels
- Never misrepresent facts or provide false information

### 2.2 Respect
- Treat all colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity
- Value diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- Practice active listening in all interactions

### 2.3 Excellence
- Strive for high quality in all work deliverables
- Continuously learn and improve skills
- Take ownership of responsibilities

### 2.4 Collaboration
- Work as a team to achieve common goals
- Share knowledge and help colleagues
- Communicate openly and constructively

## 3. Professional Behavior

### 3.1 Workplace Conduct
- Maintain professional demeanor at all times
- Dress appropriately (business casual unless client-facing)
- Keep workspaces clean and organized
- Minimize personal phone usage during work hours

### 3.2 Communication Standards
- Use professional language in all communications
- Respond to emails within 24 hours (business days)
- Be punctual for meetings
- Come prepared to discussions

### 3.3 Confidentiality
- Protect company and client confidential information
- Never share login credentials
- Report security breaches immediately
- Sign NDAs as required

## 4. Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination:

### 4.1 Harassment
- Sexual harassment of any kind
- Bullying or intimidation
- Discrimination based on race, gender, religion, age, disability, or sexual orientation
- Hostile work environment creation

### 4.2 Substance Abuse
- Alcohol consumption during work hours
- Drug use on company premises
- Coming to work under the influence

### 4.3 Fraud and Theft
- Misappropriation of company funds
- Theft of company or colleague property
- Falsifying records or reports
- Fraudulent expense claims

### 4.4 Conflict of Interest
- Engaging in competing business
- Accepting inappropriate gifts (>â‚¹5,000 value)
- Making hiring decisions involving relatives without disclosure

## 5. Digital Conduct

### 5.1 Email and Internet Usage
- Company email is for business purposes primarily
- Limited personal internet usage permitted during breaks
- No accessing inappropriate content
- No downloading unauthorized software

### 5.2 Social Media
- Do not represent company views without authorization
- Do not share confidential information
- Do not post negative content about the company
- Maintain professional online presence

## 6. Reporting Violations

### 6.1 Reporting Channels
- Direct manager
- HR department
- Ethics hotline: ethics@company.com
- Anonymous reporting portal

### 6.2 Non-Retaliation
- Whistleblowers are protected from retaliation
- All reports are treated confidentially
- False reports made in bad faith are subject to disciplinary action

## 7. Disciplinary Actions

| Violation Level | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Minor | Verbal warning | Written warning | Suspension |
| Major | Written warning | Suspension | Termination |
| Severe | Immediate termination | N/A | N/A |

## 8. Acknowledgment

All employees must sign an acknowledgment form indicating they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Code of Conduct.
